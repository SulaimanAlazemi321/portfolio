About Me

I'm a Computer Science student with a strong interest in Cybersecurity, currently studying at the University of Salford. I enjoy learning new skills and tackling practical challenges, always looking to grow my knowledge in this exciting field.

During my internship in Network Solutions, I had the chance to work alongside cybersecurity professionals, gaining hands-on experience with tools such as Proofpoint, Forcepoint, CrowdStrike, and SealPath. I also designed and delivered beginner-friendly courses in cybersecurity and ethical hacking, helping others understand important topics like Kali Linux, NMAP, and WiFi security.

I've earned certifications including CompTIA A+, Network+, and Security+, which have given me a solid foundation in both technical and theoretical aspects of cybersecurity. Additionally, I enjoy practicing through Capture The Flag (CTF) challenges on platforms like TryHackMe and HackTheBox, where I've learned both offensive and defensive security techniques.

I'm currently exploring the role of machine learning in cybersecurity, particularly for vulnerability assessment and threat detection. I'm always keen to connect with others who share my interest in cybersecurity, whether they're fellow students, professionals, or mentors.
