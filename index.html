About Me

I'm a Computer Science student with a strong interest in Cybersecurity, currently studying at the University of Salford. I enjoy learning new skills and tackling practical challenges, always looking to grow my knowledge in this exciting field.

During my internship in Network Solutions, I had the chance to work alongside cybersecurity professionals, gaining hands-on experience with tools such as Proofpoint, Forcepoint, CrowdStrike, and SealPath. I also designed and delivered beginner-friendly courses in cybersecurity and ethical hacking, helping others understand important topics like Kali Linux, NMAP, and WiFi security.

I've earned certifications including CompTIA A+, Network+, and Security+, providing me with a solid foundation in both technical and theoretical aspects of cybersecurity. Additionally, I regularly engage with Capture The Flag (CTF) challenges on platforms like TryHackMe and HackTheBox, enhancing my skills in both offensive and defensive cybersecurity practices.

Currently, I'm expanding my knowledge by learning about security solutions and configuring technologies such as Data Loss Prevention (DLP), Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR), Extended Detection and Response (XDR), Network Detection and Response (NDR), Two-Factor Authentication (2FA), DNS Security Extensions (DNSSEC), and more.

I'm always keen to connect with others who share my passion for cybersecurity, whether they're fellow students, professionals, or mentors. Feel free to reach out and connect!
